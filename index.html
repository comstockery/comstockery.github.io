Sample highlight: Mantronix "King of the Beats" (1988)

The siren heard throughout Donuts comes from hip-hop and electro-funk group Mantronix's "King of the Beats." Stereogum published a longer history of "King of the Beats" in 2019.

J Dilla used the Mantronix siren as his producer's tag—a short sound used as a producer's signature. J Dilla first used the Mantronix siren on "Let's Take it Back", a track on his 2003 EP Ruff Draft.

"King of the Beats" contains 12 known samples. The siren we hear in Donuts is from Adams & Fleisner, an alias of a Dutch production team known as Cat Music, who released an album of digital effects in 1984. The same siren has been recently used in songs by Kendrick Lamar, Tyler the Creator, and The Weeknd.
